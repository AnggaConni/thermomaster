Understanding Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics is the branch of physics that deals with heat, work, and temperature, and their relation to energy, radiation, and physical properties of matter.

The Fundamental Laws

Zeroth Law

"The Law of Thermal Equilibrium"

If two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third system, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other. This allows us to define temperature.

If $A = C$ and $B = C$, then $A = B$

First Law

"The Law of Conservation of Energy"

Energy can be converted from one form to another with the interaction of heat, work and internal energy, but it cannot be created or destroyed.

$\Delta U = Q - W$

Second Law

"The Law of Entropy"

The total entropy of an isolated system can never decrease over time. Heat naturally flows from hot objects to cold objects, not the other way around.

$\Delta S_{total} \geq 0$

Third Law

"The Law of Absolute Zero"

As temperature approaches absolute zero ($0$ Kelvin), the entropy of a system approaches a constant minimum value. Perfect order is achieved.

$S \to 0$ as $T \to 0$ K

Practical Case: CPU Cooling

Why it matters?

Your CPU generates intense heat through electrical resistance (First Law: electrical energy becomes thermal energy). To prevent damage, we must move this heat to a heatsink (Second Law: heat flows from the hot CPU to the cooler heatsink).

Thermodynamic Concept

Microscopically, the surfaces of a CPU and Heatsink are uneven. Air gaps act as insulators. Thermal paste (TIM) acts as a high-conductivity bridge, maximizing thermal contact.

Thermal Paste Guide

1

Cleaning

Remove old paste using Isopropyl Alcohol (90%+) and a lint-free cloth.

2

Application

Apply a "pea-sized" amount of high-quality paste in the center of the CPU IHS.

3

Pressure

Mount the cooler. The pressure spreads the paste evenly, eliminating air pockets (entropy reduction in the contact layer).
